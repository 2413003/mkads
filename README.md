# mkads
ad platform better for consumers, advertisers, venues/hosts. (all key stakeholders)

consumers: less quantity, higher relevance, only actually truly helpful ads for things they actually truly want that solve actual real problems they have. Big highest priority/value problems. Not invasive/annoying. Actually helpful & useful and something people actually like, not dislike & put up with/try to skip. makes local events more sustainable & supported at no extra cost to consumers. lowering prices & supporting community venues & events alive at no extra cost.

for advertisers: higher ROI. ideally more profitable. supporting community. 

venues: monetise their property (ad rev shared), grow revenue from existing property at no extra cost



can longterm be platform letting you buy ANY ads anywhere. like ad platform making all existing ad platforms/companies compete. like you can buy & manage & track meta, google, local council, local, digital & physical ads anywhere & anyhow in 1 place. with reputation of each from other advertisers, ROI estimations etc. taking away monopoly of ad companies. making them compete under 1 platform selling their commodity. lowering cost & improving results for advertisers. 




relevance for consumers: e.g. rather than just constantly trying to sell people on stuff they don't want, it could be that poor people get actually useful job/career ads, rich people get product/service ads for their level of richness, people looking for X actually get ads for X, and not Y. unless they've indicated or there's a high chance they truly really want Y. if there's not a high chance people really want something, don't show them an ad for it. it just wastes advertiser money and is annoying for user. it only benefits the ad monopoly like meta ads/google ads but they get away with it because they have monopoly on ads. not competing with an actually good ad platform that are actually good for advertisers and consumers. another example is ads where only commission is charged. so there's no risk for advertisers. so for things people can buy through mk group owned platform. like imagine something's actually truly useful for you, and you can buy it there and then and ad platform takes commission and advertiser gets sale and consumer benefits from getting something they really truly want, so everyone is benefitted.



Also longterm should be possible for advertisers to just click a button and they don’t need to do anything, mk ads just extracts all existing info about their product/service(s) from a website given like amazon listing or ebay listing or deliveroo listing etc or their shopify website or woocommerce website and it then pulls this info immediately and asks advertiser to confirm if this is correct, and they can edit it themselves if not entirely.
